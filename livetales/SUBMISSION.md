# LiveTales

**Where every doodle becomes a tale.**

---

## Project Summary

LiveTales is an interactive AI storytelling app for children ages 3–8. Kids draw on a canvas and talk in real-time with **Tali** — an AI friend who sounds like an excited 7-year-old, built as an agent using the **Google Agent Development Kit (ADK)**. Tali watches the child draw, reacts with genuine enthusiasm, asks imaginative questions, and helps co-create a story. When the child is ready, LiveTales turns their drawings and conversation into a fully illustrated, narrated, animated 6-page storybook.

The core idea: **turn passive screen time into active creative play** — powered entirely by Google's AI stack.

---

## Features & Functionality

**Tali — The ADK-Powered Voice Agent**
Tali is the heart of LiveTales. She's defined as a Google ADK `Agent` with a personality-driven system prompt ("You are Tali, a fun and curious kid...") and runs via ADK's `Runner.run_live()` with bidirectional audio streaming. ADK manages her session state, streaming lifecycle, voice configuration, and turn-taking — making her feel like a real friend, not a chatbot. She never breaks character: if you ask who she is, she just says "I'm Tali!"

**Real-Time Voice Conversation**
Children speak naturally into the microphone and hear Tali respond instantly through Gemini 2.0 Flash Live's native audio streaming. The conversation flows naturally — Tali can be interrupted mid-sentence and picks back up gracefully, just like a real conversation.

**Interactive Drawing Canvas**
A full HTML5 drawing canvas with 8 colors, brush sizes, undo, and clear. Canvas snapshots are sent to the backend every 5 seconds, so Tali can "see" what the child is drawing and react to it in real-time ("Ooh is that a dragon? I love dragons!").

**AI Story Generation**
On demand, Gemini 2.5 Flash receives the child's drawing (as an image) plus the full conversation context between Tali and the child, and generates a structured 6-page children's story — from "Once upon a time..." to a warm, uplifting ending. Each page includes narrative text and a detailed illustration prompt.

**AI Illustration**
Each story page gets a watercolor-style children's storybook illustration generated by Imagen 3.0, with child-safe content filters (`safety_filter_level: block_low_and_above`).

**Narrated Storybook**
Gemini 2.5 Flash TTS reads the complete story warmly and expressively — like a fun friend telling a bedtime story — using the Aoede voice.

**Animated Story Video**
Veo 2.0 takes the first illustration and animates it into a gentle, dreamy video clip, creating a shareable animated storybook experience.

## Data Sources

No external datasets. Everything is generated in real-time from two inputs:
- **Child's voice** — microphone audio (PCM 16kHz) transcribed by Gemini
- **Child's drawing** — canvas snapshots (JPEG) analyzed by Gemini's vision

All outputs — story text, illustrations, narration audio, animated video — are produced on-the-fly by Google AI models. Every story is unique because it comes from the child's own imagination.

## Findings & Learnings

**1. ADK makes real-time voice agents accessible.**
Building a bidirectional voice agent from scratch would be daunting. ADK's `Agent` + `Runner` + `LiveRequestQueue` pattern gave us production-grade streaming with minimal boilerplate. We defined Tali's personality as an ADK Agent, configured `StreamingMode.BIDI` with voice settings, and ADK handled the rest — session management, turn-taking, audio transcription, interruption handling. The key architectural pattern was three concurrent async tasks in a `TaskGroup`: one receiving from the WebSocket, one feeding audio to ADK's `LiveRequestQueue`, and one processing events from `Runner.run_live()`.

**2. Decouple live streaming from heavy generation.**
Voice (Gemini Live via ADK) needs to stay responsive at all times. Story generation (Gemini 2.5 Flash), illustration (Imagen 3.0), and video (Veo 2.0) are heavier calls that run asynchronously in the background. Keeping these as separate async pipelines — live voice through ADK, everything else through a dedicated Vertex AI client — meant Tali never "freezes" while a story page is being generated.

**3. Latency is everything when your user is 5 years old.**
Children lose attention in seconds. Using `gemini-2.0-flash-live-preview` for voice gave sub-second response times. Offloading story + illustration to async background tasks let us return story pages progressively — text first, then illustration — so the child always has something to look at.

**4. Conversation context makes stories personal.**
We capture both Tali's and the child's speech transcriptions (tagged as `Tali:` and `Child:`) into a rolling buffer. This conversation context, combined with the canvas snapshot, is what makes each generated story deeply personal — the story references what the child actually said and drew.

**5. Child safety requires defense in depth.**
Imagen's safety filters, carefully scoped prompts ("watercolor style, warm colors, safe for children ages 3-8"), Tali's instruction to "never be scary or negative," and the `block_low_and_above` filter level all work together. No single layer is enough — it's the combination that keeps content age-appropriate.
